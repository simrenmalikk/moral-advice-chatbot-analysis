# Moral Advice from Chatbots: A Small Exploratory Analysis

## Research Question
How do chatbot responses to moral dilemmas differ in terms of tone, certainty, and type of advice?

## Overview
This project explores how a language model responds to moral dilemmas. The goal is to analyze whether chatbot answers tend to be direct, cautious, balanced, or restrictive.

## Dataset
The dataset consists of 10 manually selected moral dilemmas reflecting ethical conflicts such as honesty, harm, fairness, privacy, and responsibility.

## Method
- Generated chatbot-style responses to each dilemma
- Applied rule-based classification to categorize response tone:
  - Direct
  - Balanced
  - Restrictive
  - Cautious
  - Uncertain

## Results
- Balanced: 4
- Restrictive: 3
- Cautious: 2
- Direct: 1

## Interpretation
Most chatbot responses are balanced or cautious, indicating a tendency to avoid strong or absolute moral judgments. Instead, responses often provide nuanced perspectives and conditional reasoning.

This pattern may reflect alignment strategies in AI systems, where models are optimized to avoid harmful or overly prescriptive advice.

## Limitations
- Small manually created dataset
- Simplified rule-based tone classification
- No comparison across multiple AI systems

## Future Work
- Analyze responses from multiple AI models
- Use more advanced NLP techniques for classification
- Collect real-world user interaction data
